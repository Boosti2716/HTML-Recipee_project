Lets make some enchiladas

Enchiladas are a traditional Mexican dish made with tortillas rolled around a filling and covered in a savory sauce. They can be filled with a variety of ingredients, including meats, cheese, beans, vegetables, or combinations. Enchiladas are typically baked until the cheese is melted and bubbly, and are often served with sour cream, guacamole, and salsa. Enchiladas are a delicious and versatile dish that can be enjoyed by people of all ages. They are perfect for a casual meal or a special occasion.

Ingredients

  • Enchilada sauce
  • Tortillas (corn or flour) (Dont use flour tortillas!)
  • Filling(cooked meat, beans, cheese, and seasonings)
  • Shredded cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.

  2. Prepare the filling. You can use store-bought enchilada filling or make your own. If making your own, simply combine cooked meat, beans, cheese, and your desired seasonings.

  3. Warm the tortillas. If using corn tortillas, wrap them in a damp paper towel and microwave for 30 seconds. If using flour tortillas, brush them with a little bit of oil and heat them in a skillet over medium heat for a few seconds on each side.

  4. Assemble the enchiladas. Spread a layer of enchilada sauce in the bottom of a baking dish. Then, fill each tortilla with the desired amount of filling and roll it up. Place the rolled enchiladas seam-side down in the baking dish.

  5. Top with sauce and cheese. Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the enchiladas, then top with shredded cheese.

  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

  7. Enjoy! Serve the enchiladas with your favorite toppings, such as sour cream, guacamole, and salsa.
